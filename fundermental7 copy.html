KNOW GOD KNOW LIFE NO GOD NO LIFE

FUNDAMENTAL BELIEF 7:
NATURE OF MAN


Man and woman were made in the image of God with individuality, the power and freedom to think and do. Though created free beings, each is an indivisible unity of body, mind and spirit dependent upon God for life and breath and all else. When our first parents disobeyed God, they denied their dependence upon Him and fell from their high position under God. The image of God in them was marred and they became subject to death. But God sent Jesus to die, to reconcile and restore man into the image of their maker .

When we look at the choices we make in life, even the small daily choices, it’s easy to feel like we’re an untidy mix of contradictions.  We can spend the whole day at a job where we help others, then come home and snap at a family member for no particular reason. We can try our hardest to be kind and patient with someone we love, only to say something harsh to them at the worst possible moment. We are capable of great empathy and selflessness—and we are also capable of cruelty, hatred, and insensitivity.
Why are we like this?
Were we always like this?
Will we forever be like this?
In the next two lessons, we will look to the Bible to tell us who we are. The Bible will give us the whole story of where we came from, and why we are the way we are.



We’ll unpack from the Bible about:
Man’s or Humanity’s origin
What it means to be created in God’s image
How humanity lost their perfection, severing their connection to God
How that loss of connection impacts every part of our lives
God’s plan to redeem us and repair the relationship we once had with Him

The Origin of Man

What is the origin of man?
God said “Let Us (the Godhead) Man was the crowning event of creation after which God rested. Gen 1:26-27; 2:7-8.
Adam is the son of God. Luke 3:38
From what was man created?
God formed man from the dust of the ground. He molded man and then breathed into him the breath of life. Gen 2:7
Man has an exalted position in relation to all other creation. God made him in charge of Planet Earth and all animal life. Gen 1:28; Ps.8:4-9 (Powerful!)
The human race is united as we all came out of the hand of God. Acts. 17:26. (Powerful!)
What are the characteristic parts of human beings?
God first formed the human being from the dust of the earth and then He put in him breath or spark of life - Gen.2:7; Job 33:4 (Wow!)
So, the dust of the ground + the breath of life = living being or living soul. We owe all to GOD!

What does it mean that man was created in the image and likeness of God?
Gen.1:27. God is a personal being. Some people did see some of His person
Exo.24:9-11 (feet); Exo.33:20-23 (back); Dan 7:9,10 (seated on the throne); Col. 1:15; Heb. 1:3 (Jesus)
Man was created in the moral image of God.
We can choose whether to obey and love God or to distrust or disobey. 1 John 4:8; Matt.22:36-40.

The Origin of Man
Man was created for relationship with others. God said, “It is not good that man should be alone” Gen.2:18. To be genuinely human is to be relationship oriented, with God and with others - just like the Godhead.
God created man to be Stewards of the environment and rule over the rest of created beings. Gen 1:26; Ps 8:6-8. Here, man’s exalted position was indicative of the glory and honor with which he was crowned. Ps.8:5.
Man was created to imitate God. We are called to reflect our Maker within our areas of dominion. For example, we follow our Maker’s example when we work for the first six days of the week and then rest on the seventh. Exo.20:8-11
God created man with conditional immortality
At creation, our first parents were given immortality, thou their possession of it was conditioned upon obedience. Disobedience would lead to death. Gen 2:15-17; 3:22

The Fall of Man
Though created perfect and in God’s image, and placed in a perfect environment, Adam and Eve became transgressors.
How did such a radical and terrible transformation come about?
If God created a perfect world, how could sin develop?
God and the origin of sin – Is God the Creator also the author of sin?
Isa.6:3; Deut.32:4; Job 34:10; God hates sin Ps. 5:4; 11:5.
If God is not the author of sin, who then is its author?
God created beings with the power of choice. One of His created beings, Lucifer out of pride exercised his freedom of choice. Eze.28:17; 1 Tim 3:6; he coveted God’s own place Isa.14:12-14. He sowed seeds of discord among fellow angels and fell with a third of them. Rev. 12:4, 7-9.
The origin of sin in the human race
Satan enticed the newly created human race to join him in the rebellion against God’s government Gen.3:4,5.
He created doubt in Eve and a break down in faith in God and His Word Gen. 3:6.
What was the immediate impact and consequences of sin? Gen 3:7 (shame); Adam and Even were afraid of God Gen 3:8-10 ; Instead of admitting their sin they engaged in blame game Gen 3:12,13; a curse was pronounced by God Gen 3:16-19 !
So then what is sin?
1 John 3:4; James 4:17;
Rom 14:23; John 16:9 Sin is rebellion against God and His will. Any sin, small or great results in the verdict “guilty”
Sin involves not only visible acts of disobedience but also our thoughts as well Exo 20:13,14
Sin produces guilt One who has committed sin is liable to punishment Rom 3:19 and if not unresolved sin produces death Rom 6:23

What is the cure for guilt?
Forgiveness! Matt.6:12 which brings to the sinner peace of mind. Jesus invites us to bring our sins to Him Matt.11:28-30. The universal sinfulness of humanity Rom. 3:23; 1 John 1:9; In Adam all die; 1 Cor 15:22 and Rom 5:12 because all sinned! By nature we tend towards evil not good.
Can you eradicate sin from your life? John 8:36 ; John 15:4,5.
We need divine power to have victory over sin. 2 Cor.5:17: 1 John 2:1
